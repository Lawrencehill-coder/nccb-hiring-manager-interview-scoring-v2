How to Use This Form

Purpose. This form scores the Second Interview and Final Decision stages for two roles at NCCB: Sales Manager / Floor Supervisor and Debt Counselor / Sales Rep. The First Interview is the phone screen, completed on the separate Phone Interview form and carried into this one automatically. It replaces judgment calls with a consistent, weighted scoring model so two different interviewers reach the same conclusion from the same candidate performance, and it keeps a written record of why each hiring decision was made.

Workflow

  1. Complete Candidate Information, then select the interview track: Sales Manager or Debt Counselor / Sales Rep. This determines which criteria load in Sections A and B — the two tracks are scored differently because the roles require different evidence.
  2. Score all seven criteria in Section A from 1 to 4 during or immediately after the first interview. Each row lists what a 1, 2, 3, and 4 answer actually sounds like — pick the description that best matches what the candidate said or did, not what you think they meant. The weighted total and decision band update live as you go.
  3. Check any disqualifying behavior in Section B the moment it appears, even if the numeric score above still looks good. A checked item overrides the score — the candidate should not advance regardless of how well they scored elsewhere.
  4. Run the 90-Day Survival Check (Section C) once the candidate advances to a second interview. This is a separate model from Section A — it predicts whether someone who scores well in an interview will still be performing 90 days after their start date. Two Low signals on any Critical-weighted factor auto-flags Do Not Hire regardless of the Section A score.
  5. Select the candidate's DISC profile in Section D as a secondary filter, applied only after you've seen the live role-play — behavioral style is easy to fake in the first five minutes of small talk and much harder to fake under the pressure of a role-play.
  6. Record context, direct quotes, and anything the checkboxes above don't capture in Section E. This is what a hiring manager or Dickson will read if they weren't in the room.
  7. Complete Section F at each stage. The First Interview box is the phone screen — if the candidate arrived here via an emailed link, it's already filled in and locked, read-only, showing who screened them and their recommendation. The Second Interview box is this in-person interview: the scoring matrix, disqualifiers, and question bank above are what you're completing right now. Final Decision is signed once a hiring decision is made. Each stage submits as its own row to the tracking sheet (see "Submit to Database" below), so the record shows how the recommendation evolved candidate by candidate.
  8. If this candidate did not go through a phone screen (a direct referral, for example), you can fill in the First Interview box yourself before completing the Second Interview box — it isn't locked until data actually arrives from a submitted stage.

Key Terms

Raw TotalThe sum of the seven 1–4 scores in Section A before any weighting is applied. Shown for reference only — it is not what the decision band is based on.
Weighted TotalEach criterion's raw score multiplied by that criterion's assigned weight, then summed across all seven. This is the number the Decision Band is based on.
Decision BandThe hiring recommendation range the Weighted Total falls into: Do Not Hire, Conditional (Second Interview Required), Strong (Proceed to Offer), or Elite (Fast-Track Offer). See the reference strip under Section A for the exact cutoffs.
DisqualifierA specific behavior or answer, listed in Section B, that should end consideration for this role on its own — independent of the numeric score.
90-Day Survival SignalA secondary check (Section C) run at the second interview that predicts whether a candidate who interviews well will still be performing three months into the job.
Critical / High / MediumThe weight tier assigned to each 90-Day Survival factor. It controls how much a single Low answer on that factor should worry you — two Low answers on Critical factors alone triggers an automatic Do Not Hire flag.
DISC ProfileA behavioral-style read — Dominant, Influencer, Steady, or Conscientious — applied after the role-play as a secondary filter. It is not part of the numeric score; it's a second opinion on fit.
TrackWhich criteria set applies to this candidate: Sales Manager or Debt Counselor / Sales Rep. Chosen once, near the top of the form, before scoring begins.
StageWhich point in the process a given submission represents — First Interview, Second Interview, or Final Decision. Selected in "Submit to Database" at the bottom; each stage writes its own row to the tracking sheet so the record shows the full history.

How to Use This Form

Purpose. This is the phone screening stage — the first of the two interviews this system tracks. It replaces the paper phone screening sheet with the same three questions and listening criteria, scored digitally so the result carries forward automatically instead of being retyped by hand.

Workflow

  1. Complete Candidate Information during or right after the call, including Position Applied For — this determines which scoring track (Sales Manager or Debt Counselor) the candidate's file uses once it reaches the Manager's In-Person Interview.
  2. Ask Question 1 and rate all five categories from 1 to 5. Each category shows what a low, middle, and high score actually sounds like — rate what the candidate said, not what you think they meant. The average only reflects the categories you've rated so far, and stays flagged as "not final" until all five are scored.
  3. Ask Question 2 and Question 3. Click a listen-for item to expand a quick reference showing what a strong versus weak answer sounds like, plus a suggested follow-up question if the answer sounded weak or hedged. Check the box only for items you actually heard — unchecked doesn't mean bad, it just wasn't demonstrated.
  4. The Phone Screen Score Summary near the bottom is a suggested read based on your Q1 rating and how many Q2/Q3 items were checked. It is not a rule and does not decide anything — the Recommendation dropdown below it is your call, informed by your notes.
  5. Set the Recommendation, sign, and date, then click Submit to Database.

What happens when you submit

If you select Advance to Manager's In-Person Interview, the hiring manager is automatically emailed a link that opens the Manager's In-Person Interview form with this candidate's information already filled in. Specifically, your recommendation, signature, and date populate the First Interview box of that form's sign-off section, shown there grayed out and read-only — the manager only needs to complete the Second Interview box, which is the actual in-person scoring, disqualifier check, and interview questions. If you select Hold or Do Not Advance instead, no link is sent; a notification email still goes out so there's a record, but the candidate's file stops here.

Key Terms

Q1 AverageThe average of the 1–5 ratings across all five Question 1 categories. Only counts categories you've actually rated — shown with a warning until all five are scored.
Q2 / Q3 ObservedHow many of the listen-for items you checked as actually demonstrated on the call, out of the total for that question.
Phone Screen Score SummaryA combined, plain-language read (Strong / Solid / Mixed / Weak) based on Q1's average and the Q2/Q3 observed counts. It's a suggestion to inform your judgment, not a gate — you can recommend Advance even if it reads Mixed, or Hold even if it reads Strong, based on what you actually heard.
